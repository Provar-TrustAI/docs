This tutorial walks you from a brand-new Trust AI account to your first scored evaluation of a real agent, in roughly ten minutes. It assumes you're setting Trust AI up for a customer agent for the first time; the shape is the same when you add a new agent to an existing workspace.

Most of these steps you'll drive by asking the [Trust Agent](/concepts/trust-agent) — the in-app assistant — in plain language, then watching the results land in the UI. Where you'd rather do something by hand, the manual UI path is right there too. On a brand-new project the Trust Agent is taught exactly this arc — connect, explore, create coverage, evaluate — so you can also just follow its lead.

By the end you'll have:

- A [Project](/concepts/projects) connected to your agent's platform
- Real [Sessions](/concepts/sessions) you can read end to end
- Starter [Evaluators](/concepts/evaluators) and [Scenarios](/concepts/scenarios) adopted from the Trust Library
- One scored [Evaluation](/concepts/evaluations) run you know how to read

That's one complete pass through [the evaluation loop](/concepts/evaluation-loop). Once it works once, the rest of Trust AI is "do this same thing at scale."

## Before you start

You'll need:

- A Trust AI account with access to a workspace where you can create projects
- An agent on one of the three platforms Trust AI connects to — **AWS Bedrock AgentCore**, **Salesforce Agentforce**, or **Claude Managed Agent** — plus its connection credentials, or an admin who has them
- About 10 minutes

<Tip>
  **No credentials at hand?** Start anyway. When you ask the Trust Agent to connect your agent, it hands you a plain-English, per-platform checklist of exactly what to gather — something you can forward to your admin. For AgentCore, the setup wizard produces an admin handoff package your AWS admin can deploy without you.
</Tip>

## The six steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create your first project">
    A [Project](/concepts/projects) is the workspace boundary around one agent under evaluation. Members, connections, and every artifact downstream belong to it.

    Creating a Project is the one setup step you do before the Trust Agent comes into play — the conversation lives *inside* a Project, so you need one first. Open the project switcher at the top of the sidebar and click **Create new project** (on a workspace with no projects yet, you'll land on a **Create your first project** screen with a **New project** button instead). Name it after the agent under evaluation (e.g., "Acme Support Agent") — a description is optional — and click **Create project**.

    **You should see:** you land inside the new Project. The left nav shows its surfaces: **New chat**, **Playground**, **Scenarios**, **Sessions**, **Evaluators**, **Evaluations**, **Requirements**, and **Project Settings** — all empty for now.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Connect your agent">
    Click **New chat**. On a brand-new project, the welcome screen greets you with four starter prompts that trace this exact tutorial: **Connect agent**, **Explore behaviour**, **Create scenarios**, and **Run evaluation**. Clicking one pre-fills the message box — nothing sends until you do.

    <Note>
      *Ask the Trust Agent:* "Help me connect the agent I want to test."
    </Note>

    The agent first checks whether a build is already connected. If not, its guided connect flow covers **Salesforce Agentforce** and **Claude Managed Agent**: it asks which platform you're on, gives you a plain-English checklist of the credentials to gather, then sends you to the connection form to enter them. Secrets are write-only — once saved, Trust AI never displays them back.

    Prefer the manual path, or connecting an **AWS Bedrock AgentCore** agent? Open **Project Settings**, switch to the **Connection** tab, and click **Add connection**. You'll pick from the three platforms:

    - **AWS Bedrock AgentCore** — a guided setup wizard: prepare an admin handoff package, have your AWS admin deploy the cross-account role, then paste the role ARN back. Full walkthrough: [Connect an AWS Bedrock AgentCore agent](/how-to/connect-agentcore).
    - **Salesforce Agentforce** — a form pointing at your Salesforce org (JWT sign-in via an External Client App is the recommended method).
    - **Claude Managed Agent** — a form for an Anthropic Managed Agent on Claude Platform on AWS.

    **You should see:** your agent listed as a connection on the **Connection** tab.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Observe real sessions">
    With the agent connected, pull in the conversations it's already having.

    <Note>
      *Ask the Trust Agent:* "Pull in some of my real conversations so we have material to work from."
    </Note>

    Or do it by hand: open **Sessions** and click **Sync now** — it's a per-agent dropdown, so pick your connected agent. Conversations pulled from your platform land in the list with Source **External** (a Session's [Source](/concepts/sessions) is always one of **Scenario**, **Playground**, or **External**). Alongside Source, the list shows things like **Routed To**, turn count, and date.

    Click any Session to open it. The fly-in leads with the full **Transcript** — every turn, tool calls included — plus tabs for **Annotations**, **Evaluations**, and **Details**. Read one end to end; this is the raw material everything downstream grades.

    <Tip>
      **Agent has no traffic yet?** Ask the Trust Agent: "Try my agent with a few example questions and show me how it responds." It runs an observe-only exploration — a real conversation with your connected agent, watched but not scored — so you can see its behavior before you define what "good" means.
    </Tip>

    **You should see:** the Sessions list populated, and at least one Session you've read end to end.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Adopt starter evaluators and scenarios">
    A new project doesn't start from a blank slate: the Trust Library ships curated defaults you can adopt in one move, and the Trust Agent can draft coverage specific to your agent.

    <Note>
      *Ask the Trust Agent:* "Generate a first set of test scenarios for my agent." Then: "Add some security scenarios that probe for unsafe or off-policy answers."
    </Note>

    The agent proposes [Scenarios](/concepts/scenarios) — each a persona plus a goal, the test cases your evaluations will run — and pauses with an approval card showing exactly what it will create before anything is saved. Approve, and they land in your Scenarios list.

    The manual path is the Trust Library:

    - On **Evaluators**, click **Add recommended** to adopt from the 12 recommended [Evaluators](/concepts/evaluators) — six Quality and six Safety presets, each a plain-language pass/fail judge.
    - On **Scenarios**, click **Add recommended scenarios** to adopt from the 10 starter safety scenarios — one per failure mode in the OWASP Agentic Top 10.

    Everything you adopt becomes an ordinary, fully editable evaluator or scenario — no special status.

    **You should see:** the Scenarios list populated, each scenario carrying a stable id like `SCN-000022`, and the Evaluators list showing your adopted judges.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Run your first evaluation">
    Now run those scenarios against your connected agent.

    <Note>
      *Ask the Trust Agent:* "Run my first evaluation against the connected agent."
    </Note>

    By hand: on **Scenarios**, click **Run** (select specific scenarios first, or leave the selection empty to run the current view). The evaluation launcher opens with a fan-out preview — one conversation per persona per scenario, simulated live against your connected agent, then graded by your evaluators. Confirm to launch. The same launcher also opens from **New evaluation** on the **Evaluations** page.

    You stay on the Scenarios list while the run executes: each scenario shows a live progress badge that settles as it finishes.

    **You should see:** per-scenario badges settling, and a new run appearing on the **Evaluations** page.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Read the results">
    Open the run from **Evaluations**.

    The headline is the **pass rate** — how the agent did across the run. Every persona is scored on its own, so the per-persona matrix shows you *who* the agent struggles with, not just whether it struggles. A second headline, **evaluator accuracy**, fills in once you've recorded human Pass/Fail verdicts on sessions — it measures how often the automated judges agree with a person, grading the judges rather than the agent.

    Open any result to read the full conversation — the transcript labels itself **Simulated**. And if something outside the agent broke — a guardrail block, an infrastructure hiccup — that result is marked an environment problem and excluded from the score, never counted as an agent failure.

    **You should see:** a scored run with a pass rate, per-persona results, and readable transcripts behind every verdict.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## You've walked the loop once

Connect, observe, define coverage, run, read — that's [the evaluation loop](/concepts/evaluation-loop), end to end, once. From here it's depth, not new shape: author expected outputs on the scenarios that matter, tailor the recommended evaluators to your agent's rules, and record human verdicts on sessions so evaluator accuracy has ground truth to grade against.
